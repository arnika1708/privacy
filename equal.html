EQUAL OPPORTUNITY POLICY

Emissions EV Technologies Inc. (“Emission”) is an equal opportunity employer and all employees and job applicants have the right to equal treatment with respect to employment without discrimination based on Indigenous identity, race, colour, ancestry, place of origin, political belief, religion, marital status, family status, physical or mental disability, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, age, or conviction of a criminal or summary conviction offence (if unrelated to employment or intended employment) or any other status protected by applicable law.

Emission prohibits unlawful discrimination based on Indigenous identity, race, colour, ancestry, place of origin, political belief, religion, marital status, family status, physical or mental disability, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, age, or conviction of a criminal or summary conviction offence (if unrelated to employment or intended employment) or any other status protected by applicable law. Emission is committed to compliance with all applicable laws and human rights codes in effect and is committed to providing equal employment opportunities.

As required by law, Emission will meet its legal duty to accommodate through the provision of accommodation up to the point of undue hardship for employees and job applicants under any applicable protected grounds. The duty to accommodate refers to Emission’s legal obligation, in certain circumstances, to address or correct situations involving discrimination to ensure an employee or job applicant is treated equally, up to the point of undue hardship. Undue hardship means all reasonable and practical steps.

The search for accommodation is a shared responsibility among the parties involved. Employees or applicants are requesting accommodation are responsible for:

  • ensuring Emission is aware of a request for accommodation by informing President of Operations Sam Dughestani at SD@emissionev.ca.
  • providing reasonable notice of the requirement for accommodation, whenever possible
  • providing sufficient relevant documentation to verify the requirement for the accommodation and identify the specific accommodation needs
  • cooperating in the search for and implementation of accommodation
  • providing notice of any change in personal circumstance which removes or varies the accommodation being provided


All requests for accommodation are to be submitted in writing with the appropriate supporting documentation for consideration and/or review. The request should provide as many specifics as possible as to what is needed. The review of the request may, at the discretion of Emission, include an evaluation and determination of the scope of the accommodation and, if appropriate, requests for additional documentation, examinations, and/or opinions
